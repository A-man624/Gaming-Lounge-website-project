Esports in a Nutshell

  • Esports stands for "Electronic Sports"
  • Professional players individually, or collectively as teams face off against others depending on the game.
  • Typically, fighting games are played between individuals, wheras more team based games such as Overwatch are played between teams.
  • It is common for winnings of tournaments at the highest level to go upwards of hundreds of thousands, if not millions of dolalrs.

People in Esports

  • Scump - Call of Duty player for Optic Gaming. 1 time Call of Duty League champion, $675,000 in approximate earnings.
  • Faker - League of Legends player for T1. 2016 League of Legends World Championship MVP, $1,250,000 in approximate earnings.
  • Coldzera - Counter Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO) player for FaZe. MLG Columbus 2016 champion, $1,028,000 in approximate earnings.
  • Pengu - Rainbow Six Seige player for G2. Six Invitational 2019 champion, $376,000 in approximate earnings
  • Bugha - Fortnite player for Sentinals. Fortnite World Cup 2019 winner, $3,069,000 in approximate earnings.
